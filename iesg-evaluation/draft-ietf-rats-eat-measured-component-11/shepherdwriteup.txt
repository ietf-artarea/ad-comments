# Document History

1. Does the working group (WG) consensus represent the strong concurrence of a
few individuals, with others being silent, or did it reach broad agreement?

The document has received reviews and feedback from at least 10 people involved
in the RATS WG, across multiple channels (IETF meetings, mailing list, Github
issues). The reviewer group spans a wide range of backgrounds (academic,
confidential computing, networking infrastructure, IoT). All sections of the
document have been refined through the review process.

There is a strong consensus among interested participants.

2. Was there controversy about particular points, or were there decisions where
the consensus was particularly rough?

The only lengthy discussion which didn't lead to a single unanimous decision
was the naming of "authorities" (formerly "signers") in the information model
[signers-renaming] [signer-section]. Renaming was needed because most (if not
all) reviewers displayed some level of confusion as to the semantics of the
field. The original term - "signers" - seemed to bias readers towards assuming
the field indicated entities who had signed the Measured Component, not those
who signed the component itself to authorize its installation or use.

No other discussions caused controversy, or needed rough consensus for progress
to be made.

# Additional Reviews

5. Do the contents of this document closely interact with technologies in other
IETF working groups or external organizations, and would it therefore benefit
from their review? Have those reviews occurred? If yes, describe which
reviews took place.

The scope of the document can be broken up into a generic information model,
and an EAT-specific data model.

The data model has limited scope outside the RATS WG.

The information model could in theory be used more broadly, both in other IETF
WGs or in other SDOs, to convey the components of a system. For example, the
issue of compatibility with the TCG-defined Canonical Event Log has been
brought up by a member of the RATS WG. Direct engagement with the TCG on this
topic showed limited interest on their side in use of the info model. This
position has also been summarised on the mailing list [tcg-summary].

Other external reviews of the information model (with a goal of inclusion in
another data format) have not been forthcoming.
